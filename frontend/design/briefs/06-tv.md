# Design Brief — TV Leaderboard (`/tv`)

Status: **confirmed** (impeccable shape, 2026-07-11)

## 1. Feature Summary
The venue's unauthenticated ambient screen and the game's acquisition engine: a QR-dominant recruitment poster where a giant scannable code and pitch line own a third of the screen permanently, backed by a live top-8 scoreboard that visibly reacts to the room. Its success metric is people walking over and scanning — the scoreboard drama exists to cause that.

## 2. Primary User Action
Scan the QR. The screen funnels a passer-by's eyes from "something is happening" (live beats) to "I could be on that board" (names/scores) to "here's how" (QR).

## 3. Design Direction
The loudest surface in the system, same peacock world: deep teal as stage backdrop at TV scale, warm accent framing the QR block (the screen's one "action"), answer-tile family coloring the podium trio. Scene sentence: a bored attendee 8 meters away, coffee in hand, catches motion on a TV between booths — within 3 seconds they must read one name, one score, and one instruction. Anchors: Kahoot's lobby screen (join code as hero), sports-bar score chyrons (the ticker), festival stall signage (the pitch line's voice).

## 4. Scope
Production-ready; one route (`/tv`), one composition, 16:9 landscape at 1080p (no portrait, no responsive breakpoints — it's a TV); mock fixtures with scripted score-change sequences to exercise the beats.

## 5. Layout Strategy
- **Right third — recruitment block:** join QR huge (scannable from ~5m: ≥400px, white module field, high contrast), "**Scan. Speak. Score.**" above in display type, one supporting line ("Your language wins games here").
- **Left two-thirds:** top-3 podium band (names + scores enormous, tile-family colors, crown on #1); ranks 4–8 as a single fat-type list below.
- **Bottom edge:** full-width ticker chyron.
- No other chrome — the composition never changes, only reacts.

## 6. Key States
- Default live board
- **Diff-driven beats:** score jump → row flash + "+10" chip pop; rank swap → rows slide past each other; new #1 → 2s crown handoff; new name in top-8 → "Welcome, {nickname}!" beat (client detects the new row)
- Empty board (event start): poster stands alone — "The board is empty. Be the first." with the QR doing all the work
- Ticker rotation: "{n} voice clips collected" · "{k} languages spoken" · playful lines; factory stats (cost, pass rates) deliberately excluded — they live in `/api/metrics` for pitch slides
- Poll failure: board freezes gracefully, small "reconnecting" glyph in a corner, QR unaffected (the acquisition surface never looks broken)
- Long-runtime hygiene: no accumulating DOM/animation state (it runs all day)

## 7. Interaction Model
Zero input — pure output. Polls `/api/leaderboard?top=15` every 3s (renders top-8, keeps 15 for stable diffing) and `/api/metrics` every ~10s for the ticker. All beats derive from client-side diffs of consecutive polls; beats queue and play sequentially (never parallel), each ≤2s, board always readable during any beat.

## 8. Content Requirements
- Pitch line + supporting line; ticker templates + playful line pool (~6); welcome/crown beat templates; empty-board line
- QR encodes the tunnel join URL (same generator as the queued screen's recruiting QR — shared asset)
- Ranges: 0–15 rows, 20-char nicknames (graceful truncation at TV scale), scores to 4 digits; nicknames Latin per join screen, but test one long Devanagari nickname anyway

## 9. Recommended References (build time)
`animate.md` (beat queue, row choreography), `typeset.md` (TV-scale display hierarchy — this surface pushes the type system hardest), `delight.md` (ticker voice, crown moment)

## 10. Asserted Defaults
Top-8 on screen (not 15 — poster real estate wins per hierarchy call); beats queued, never parallel; the ≤6rem display ceiling is a phone rule and does not apply at TV scale — podium runs bigger, tuned on a real 1080p output at build time.
